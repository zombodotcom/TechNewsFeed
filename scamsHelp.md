# Comprehensive Scam Awareness Guide

## Common Scam Red Flags - MEMORIZE THESE!

**STOP if someone:**
- Demands payment via gift cards, cryptocurrency, wire transfer, or payment apps
- Asks for personal info like Social Security number, account numbers, or passwords
- Creates urgency with threats of arrest, account suspension, or service disconnection
- Claims to be from a legitimate organization but uses pressure tactics
- Asks you to keep it secret or stay off the internet
- Says you won something you never entered
- Wants you to pay fees to claim a prize or grant

---

## Recent & Trending Scams (2025)

### The Military Bank Phone Scam ⚠️ **NEW**
**"Can I borrow your phone to send money for groceries?"**

Outside military banks like USAA or Navy Federal, someone approaches with a sob story—can't access accounts, needs grocery money, may have a crying child. Once you open your banking app to help, they grab your phone and drain ALL your accounts, not just one.

**How to avoid:** Never hand your unlocked phone to strangers, especially with banking apps open.

---

### The FTC Impersonator Scam
**"This is John Krebs from the FTC. You need to move your money to protect it."**

Scammers impersonate FTC officials and government employees. They may use real names and titles to sound credible.

**Reality check:** The FTC will NEVER call and tell you to move money, withdraw cash, or buy gold. Government agencies don't threaten arrest over the phone.

---

### The Virtual Casting Call Scam
**"Congratulations! You've been selected for a virtual audition. Click here."**

Unexpected texts about casting calls, modeling gigs, or entertainment opportunities lead to phishing sites that steal personal information or demand upfront fees.

**Red flag:** Legitimate casting calls don't come via random text messages.

---

### The Reshipping Scam (Fake Work-From-Home)
**"Easy $3,000/month! Work as our Delivery Operations Specialist."**

"Job" requires you to receive packages at home, repackage them, and ship to new addresses. You're actually helping criminals launder stolen goods. When police investigate, YOUR name and address are on the shipments.

**Warning:** Never accept jobs that involve receiving and reshipping packages.

---

### The "Agent" Scam
**"Don't talk to anyone. Stay off the internet until we fix this."**

Caller claims your identity is linked to crimes and your money is at risk. They connect you to an "agent" who insists you stay silent and offline while "helping" you.

**Why the secrecy?** Because the internet would immediately tell you it's a scam. Real investigators never tell you to avoid seeking help.

## Classic Scams That Still Work

### The Fake Check Scam
Someone "overpays" you with a check and asks you to refund the difference. Their check bounces, but your refund was real money.

**Rule:** Never accept overpayment. Never send money back before checks fully clear (can take weeks).

---

### The Apartment/Rental Scam
**"I'm overseas on a mission trip. Send a deposit to hold the apartment."**

Listings on Craigslist or Facebook with great prices. "Landlord" can't meet in person but pushes for immediate deposit. The property isn't actually theirs to rent.

**Protect yourself:** ALWAYS tour properties in person. Never send money to someone you haven't met.

---

### The Fake Vehicle History Report Scam
When selling a car, "buyer" asks you to get a vehicle history report from a specific website. The site is fake—they collect your credit card info.

**Solution:** Only use established services like Carfax. Never use links sent by buyers.

---

### The Phishing Link Scam
Texts, emails, or social media messages with urgent links about package delivery, account suspension, prize winning, or security alerts.

**Before clicking:** Go directly to the official website or app. Never click links in unexpected messages.

---

### The Fake Online Retailer Scam
Social media ads featuring brand-name products at impossibly low prices. Sites look professional but disappear after taking your money.

**Protection:** 
- Check domain age (newly registered = suspicious)
- Look for contact information and physical address
- Search "[company name] scam" before buying
- Use credit cards (better fraud protection than debit)

---

### The Catfish/Online Romance Scam
Someone builds a relationship online over weeks/months. Once trust is established, they have an "emergency" and need money.

**Warning signs:**
- Refuses video calls or in-person meetings
- Stories don't add up
- Asks for money, gift cards, or cryptocurrency
- Claims to be military, working overseas, or wealthy but temporarily unable to access funds

**Remember:** If you've never met in person, don't send money.

---

### The Fake Charity Scam
After disasters or tragedies, fake charities with names similar to real organizations ask for donations with urgent pressure.

**Before donating:**
- Research on CharityNavigator.org or GuideStar.org
- Never donate through links in messages
- Use official charity websites
- Be suspicious of cash-only or gift card requests

---

### The Cryptocurrency Investment Scam
**"Guaranteed returns! Invest in crypto now!"**

Promises of unrealistic returns, "exclusive opportunities," or platforms that won't let you withdraw your money.

**Reality:** Legitimate investments never guarantee returns. If it sounds too good to be true, it is.

---

### The Fake Debt Collector Scam
Aggressive calls demanding immediate payment for debts you don't recognize.

**Your rights:**
- Ask for written validation of the debt
- Real collectors must follow legal procedures
- They can't threaten arrest or use abusive language
- You can request they only contact you in writing

---

### The Computer Pop-Up/Tech Support Scam
**"Your computer is infected! Call this number immediately!"**

Pop-ups lock your screen and display fake virus warnings. "Tech support" demands payment or remote access to "fix" it.

**Truth:** Real security alerts come from YOUR security software, not random pop-ups. Microsoft/Apple don't make unsolicited calls.

---

### The Amazon/Package Delivery Scam
**"Your package is delayed. Update your payment info."**

Texts or emails claiming to be from Amazon, USPS, FedEx, or UPS with links to "track" packages or fix delivery issues.

**What to do:** Log into your account directly (not through the link). Check order status there.

---

### The Fake Job Offer Scam
**"You're hired! Just pay for your background check/equipment/training."**

Job offers without interviews, promises of high pay for little work, or requests for money upfront.

**Red flags:**
- Payment required to get the job
- "Too good to be true" salary
- No interview process
- Poor grammar in job description
- Vague job duties

---

### The Fake Invoice/Vendor Email Scam
Business email compromise where scammers send fake invoices or impersonate vendors to get businesses to wire money.

**Prevention:**
- Verify changes to payment information by phone (using known numbers)
- Confirm large requests in person when possible
- Train employees to spot phishing

---

### The Car Warranty Scam
**"We've been trying to reach you about your car's extended warranty."**

Robocalls claiming your warranty is expiring. They collect personal information or charge for worthless "coverage."

**Fact:** Your real warranty information comes from your car dealer, not robocalls.

---

### The Jury Duty Scam
**"You missed jury duty. Pay this fine or face arrest."**

Callers claim to be court officials demanding immediate payment.

**Reality:** Courts send written notices. Fines are paid through official channels, never over the phone.

---

### The QR Code Scam
Fake QR codes placed over legitimate ones on parking meters, restaurant tables, or in public spaces. Scanning leads to phishing sites or malware.

**Safety:** Before scanning, check if the QR code looks tampered with or is a sticker placed over something else.

## Targeting Specific Groups

### Scams Against Seniors
- Grandparent scam: "Grandma, I'm in jail and need bail money. Don't tell Mom and Dad."
- Medicare card scam: Fake calls requesting Medicare number verification
- Investment scams promising safe returns
- Sweepstakes/lottery scams

### Scams Against Immigrants
- Fake immigration lawyers or consultants
- Deportation threats demanding payment
- Fake document services
- Threatening calls claiming to be from ICE or immigration officials

### Scams Against Small Businesses
- Fake invoices for services never ordered
- Directory listing scams
- Domain name renewal scams
- Google/Yelp listing scams
- Fake utility bills

## How to Protect Yourself

### BEFORE Acting on Any Request:
1. **STOP** - Don't act immediately, no matter how urgent it sounds
2. **THINK** - Does this make sense? Is this how this company/agency normally operates?
3. **VERIFY** - Contact the organization directly using official contact info YOU find

### Payment Method Red Flags:
**NEVER pay by:**
- Gift cards (iTunes, Google Play, Amazon, etc.)
- Cryptocurrency/Bitcoin
- Wire transfer (Western Union, MoneyGram)
- Payment apps to strangers (Venmo, Cash App, Zelle)
- Cash given to a courier

**Legitimate organizations accept:**
- Credit cards
- Checks payable to the organization
- Official payment portals on verified websites

### Personal Information Protection:
**NEVER share:**
- Social Security number
- Bank account numbers
- Credit card numbers
- Online banking passwords
- Medicare/insurance ID numbers

...unless YOU initiated the contact through official channels.

## What To Do If You're Scammed

### Immediate Actions:
1. **Stop contact** with the scammer immediately
2. **Document everything** - save messages, emails, caller ID info
3. **Contact your bank/credit card company** if you gave financial information
4. **Change passwords** if you shared login credentials
5. **Run antivirus software** if you clicked suspicious links

### Report the Scam:
- **FTC:** ReportFraud.ftc.gov
- **FBI Internet Crime:** IC3.gov
- **Local police** (non-emergency number)
- **Your state Attorney General**
- **Social media platform** where scam occurred
- **Your bank** if money was transferred

### Recovery Steps:
- **Place fraud alerts** on your credit reports (Equifax, Experian, TransUnion)
- **Freeze your credit** to prevent new accounts
- **Monitor accounts** for suspicious activity
- **File identity theft report** at IdentityTheft.gov if personal info was stolen

## Red Flags Summary Checklist

A communication is likely a scam if it:
- ☑️ Creates extreme urgency or fear
- ☑️ Threatens arrest, prosecution, or deportation
- ☑️ Offers something that sounds too good to be true
- ☑️ Requires payment by gift card, wire transfer, or cryptocurrency
- ☑️ Asks you to keep it secret
- ☑️ Contains spelling/grammar errors
- ☑️ Comes from a generic email (not official domain)
- ☑️ Requests remote access to your computer
- ☑️ Pressures you to act before you can think or verify
- ☑️ Claims you won something you didn't enter
- ☑️ Asks for personal information like SSN, account numbers, or passwords
